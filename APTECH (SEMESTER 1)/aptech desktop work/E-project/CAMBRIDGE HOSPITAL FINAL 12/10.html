CUH staff sweep the board at regional leadership recognition awards

CUH staff have swept the board at the East of England NHS Leadership Recognition Awards 2015, winning three of the nine categories.

Michaela Jones, the lead nurse on the John Farman Intensive Care Unit, won the inspirational leader award. Daniel Fountain, a researcher in the Brainbow neuro-rehabilitation service, won the NHS Emerging Leader of the Year and Monica Jacot, CUH equality and diversity lead, won the NHS Leader of Inclusivity of the Year. Michaela, Daniel and Monica now go through to the national finals being held in March in London.

Speciality registrar Meng Weng won the Research Poster of the Year award in the Quality in Education and Training Awards, also held on 26 November at the Imperial War Museum in Duxford.

The East of England NHS Leadership Recognition Awards celebrate the successes of healthcare professionals in the region, recognising and applauding excellence in leadership. The awards are held in partnership with Health Education East of England and NHS Leadership Academy.

Michaela said: “I am truly overwhelmed to have won. If my actions and words spark the faintest trace of inspiration in others, then it is with humility and grace that I accept the award for inspirational leader on behalf of our patients and our team on JVF ICU who inspire me. It is an honour to care at Addenbrooke’s Hospital side-by-side a fantastic team. My success is a shared success and a fabulous achievement.”
“I would like to thank Health Education East of England for my award and a special thank you to Katie Hutchinson & Petra Polgarova who took the time to nominate me.
Daniel said: “I would like to thank Katie Johnson and David Young for nominating me and to the whole Brainbow team for being fantastic to work with in developing the neuro-rehabilitation service for children with brain tumours.”

Monica said: “I am delighted and proud to have won this award. I would like to thank all the diversity champions at CUH, members of the Equality and Diversity staff group, LGBT and straight alliance, It’s Not Just You group who work together with me to promote equality, diversity and inclusion at CUH for staff and patients.
“I would also like to thank my great line manager Karen Clarke nor just for nominating me but for supporting me and trusting me to do my job.”
CUH’s interim chief operating officer, Evelyn Barker, who was also present at the awards, said: “We are very proud of Daniel, Meng, Michaela and Monica for their achievement of being recognised for outstanding leadership in healthcare.

They set excellent examples to their teams and embody the Trust’s values of being kind, safe and excellent.
“The NHS needs inspirational leaders to deal with the challenges it faces and we are delighted to have so many of them at Cambridge University Hospitals.
