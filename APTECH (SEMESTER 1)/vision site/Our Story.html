Our Success Story

It's the little things that tend to add up in a big Way!!

Gwendolyn Rogers

Gwendolyn Rogers has created a storybook restaurant in a cottage that took two and a half years for her to design & remodel. When you walk through the French doors, the sparkling chandeliers, antiqued mirrors, French inspired furniture, beautiful seasonal decorations and the rich aroma of the French chocolate cakes baking in the oven immediately greets you. Enjoy French pressed coffee with a slice of homemade cake or pie. Enjoy a cup of warm sipping chocolate topped with fresh whipped cream and a warm cookie right out of the oven. She offers an incredible lunch of ‘The Best Quiche You Will Ever Have’, homemade soups, salades, sandwiches, even a true French Croque Monsieur. The evenings offer charcuterie boards with imported meats, cheeses and olives served on elegant marble tiers. Behind the kitchen window, master bakers gently sieve non-bromated flours from Vermont, aluminum-free baking powders & sugars and large pots of French & Belgian Chocolate melting on the stove. Once the oven dings, everything at the Yame Bakery Shop has come together to create a truly sweet and savory experience. Choose from our list of the best French wines & champagnes to accompany any of our lunches, cakes, cookies, pies & desserts. Gwendolyn’s Yame Bake Shop has become the destination of Indianapolis for celebrating birthdays, anniversaries and all the special occasions life offers.


Gwendolyn and her shop have received many awards & recognition. Before opening her shop, she won ‘Best Cake’ at London’s Cake & Bake Show at the Earls Court Convention Center for her chocolate cake, she was awarded 2nd place in Valrhona’s ‘Show us Your Chocolate’ by judges Florian Bellanger & Jaques Torres, and Martha Stewarts American Made showcased her Cherry Pie.

Since her shop opening in 2014 she has been recognized for the following: Most recent award was on April 28, 2017, her shop was awarded the winner for ‘Best Bakery’ in Indiana by Indy’s A-List’ second year in a row. Preceding, Gwendolyn was voted ‘Best Apple Pie’ by Channel 4’s RTV6, voted ‘#1 Cakes in Indianapolis’ by Best Rated, voted ‘Top Spot’ to visit in Indianapolis by Top Spots. In 2016, ‘Winner Best Bakery’ in Indianapolis by Indy’s A-List, awarded ‘Certificate of Excellence’ by Trip Advisor, voted ‘Best Chocolate Chip Cookie’ in all 50 States by Tripping, ‘Best of the Midwest’ by Midwest Travel, voted ‘#1 Bakery in Indiana’ by Trip Advisor, voted ‘Top 5 Bakeries in America’ by Central Restaurant. Featured as ‘Best of Indy’ with her Millionaire Cake on the cover of Indianapolis Monthly, rated ‘Top 10 Best Things To Do’ in Indianapolis by Zagut (#2), recommended by The Conrad Hotel as ‘Top 5 Must Do’s’ while visiting Indianapolis.


In 2015, was awarded ‘Top 25 Best New Restaurants’, voted ‘Best Desserts in Indianapolis’ by Indianapolis Monthly, won ‘Best Local Pie’ by B105.7, voted ‘Coolest Places to Eat in Indy’ by Yahoo Food, named ‘Must See Destination’ by Visit Indy. Gwendolyn partnered with Kroger Stores and opened a Cake Bake Shop in their newly remodeled market in Carmel, her eclairs were featured at Le Meridian Hotel, and her cakes have been featured on the menu of St. Elmo Steakhouse.

Gwendolyn has had articles written about her in Sophisticated Living, Indianapolis Monthly and the Indianapolis Star multiple times, was featured in Go Midwest Magazine and has appeared on Fox 59, RTV6, Eat Drink Indy and the Smiley Morning Show.

Her talents have earned her national attention from many celebrities such as Paula Deen (her 70th birthday cake), Matt Damon, Jim Carrey, Ina Garten, Steve Martin, Martin Short, T.J. Miller, Allison Janney, Buddy Valastro, Marc Summers, Michael Symon, Mario Batali, Andrew Luck (Indianapolis Colts), Matt Hasselbeck (ESPN), Ron Livingston (Office Space, Loudermilk), James Hinchcliffe (Indy driver), Alexander Rossi (Indianapolis 500 Winner 2016),and most recently Thomas Keller of French Laundry & Per Se and Robert Irvine from Food Networks ‘Dinner Impossible & ‘Restaurant Impossible’. Gwendolyn was nominated for the Starkey ‘Entrepreneurial Woman Award’, and is also a part of Simply Good Food TV in England with Peter Sidwell.


Gwendolyn has been an inspiration to many and has spoken at numerous events such as Edge Mentoring (twice), Del Woman’s Leadership, and Indiana Design Centers ‘Setting The Table For Good Design’. She has even walked the runway to help raise money for ‘Dress For Success’, helping place women in the workforce. She has been featured on Food Networks ‘Guilty Pleasures’, Hallmark’s Home & Family making her Chocolate Silk Pie and joined Marc Summers on the tasting stools of ‘The Chew’. Her cakes have been in such films as ‘Man Down’with Shia LaBeouf, ‘Dumb & Dumber To’ w/ Jim Carrey and Jeff Daniels & ‘My All American’ with Aaron Eckhart.

Gwendolyn just finished working with Lenox for her own line of fine china designed exclusively for the Cake Bake Shop. French rolling pins are available for sale, they are made for her in Dorset, Vermont. ‘Pixie Glitter’ (TM) & ‘Champagne Glitters’ (TM) are Gwendolyn’s new items that she will offer to all of her customers, available in custom made pretty pink jars. She has her own wine label ‘Doberge’ which is sold exclusively in her shop and has a line called Silver Celebrations London, designed with her design partner Vivi in London, that designs silver candle holders for cakes. All items Gwendolyn makes are available for all of her customers. Gwendolyn is now working on opening her second Cake Bake Shop, announcing location soon.
